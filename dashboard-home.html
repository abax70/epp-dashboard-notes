EPP Dashboard Home Landing page and state-level overview of North Carolina educator preparation program measures

Overview

This is the landing page for NCDPI’s Educator Preparation Program dashboards. It serves two purposes:

  • Navigation — Click any chart or the tabs in the top banner to go to that measure’s detailed dashboard, where you can disaggregate results by EPP, candidate demographics, and other subgroups.
  • State-level trends — Each chart shows approximately five years of statewide results for one of 12 measures. Charts are shaded blue for increases over time, orange for decreases, and gray for stable trends.

The 12 Measures

The dashboard tracks 12 measures across three categories:

Category Measures
Pipeline Total Enrollment, New Enrollment, Completions, Initial Licenses
Outcomes Employment, Early-Career Retention, EVAAS (Student Growth), NCEES (Teaching Performance)
Quality Candidate Satisfaction, Employer Satisfaction, License Exams, License Conversions

Each measure links to a detailed dashboard with its own notes explaining the data and business rules. Hover over any card to see a brief description.

Who Is Included

Unless otherwise noted, the data reflects candidates seeking initial teaching licenses. See the Glossary for how this population is defined.

Reading the Charts

For rate-based measures (e.g., employment, pass rates), very high values (>95%) and very low values (<5%) are masked to protect small groups. When a group has fewer than ten candidates, the result is suppressed entirely and displayed as “N<10.”

Tip: Some measures use a time shift to align with program outcomes. For example, the Employment measure shows data one year after the completion year, and Retention shows data two years after, because candidates need time to enter and remain in the workforce.

Data Source

The primary source is the roster of candidates provided annually to NCDPI by the EPPs. NCDPI links this data to its own payroll, licensure, evaluation, and student growth databases. Survey data and program information also come from the EPPs.
