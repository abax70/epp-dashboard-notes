Student Growth (EVAAS) Impact of EPP completers on student growth as measured by EVAAS

Overview

This dashboard shows the impact of EPP completers on student growth as measured by the Education Value-Added Assessment System (EVAAS). Based on the test performance of a candidate’s students, each score is categorized as:

  • Did not meet expected growth
  • Met expected growth
  • Exceeded expected growth

This measure applies only to candidates teaching in a grade and subject with NC standardized testing.

Who Is Included

Candidates with initial teaching licenses (see Glossary) who completed their EPP and taught a tested grade/subject in a North Carolina traditional or charter school within three years of program completion.

How the Three-Year Window Works

After completing their EPP, a candidate has up to three years to receive EVAAS scores. The dashboard uses only the first year in which the candidate received scores. If a candidate completed in 2020 and first received EVAAS scores in 2022, only the 2022 scores appear.

EVAAS scores received by alternate-route candidates prior to their program completion are not included.

Data for recent completers may update: Because the dashboard uses a three-year window, data for recent completion cohorts may change as more candidates from those cohorts receive their first EVAAS scores in subsequent years.

COVID-19: No EVAAS data is available for 2020 or 2021. Candidates who would have received their first scores in those years appear in the dashboard under a later year instead, if applicable.

Multiple Scores per Candidate

Teachers can receive multiple EVAAS scores in a given year—one for each subject and grade they teach (e.g., Math Grade 4 and Math Grade 5). Only scores in subjects and grades pertinent to the candidate’s EPP preparation are included. Scores in subjects or grades the candidate was not trained for are excluded.

ESL and EC teachers: These teachers receive an EVAAS score only if they are designated the teacher of record for their students during the EVAAS roster validation process. When they do, the dashboard reports their composite score across grades and subjects.

Data Source

Candidate rosters are provided annually by EPPs to NCDPI. EVAAS scores are computed by SAS.

Small Counts

Results are not displayed for any group with fewer than ten candidates.
