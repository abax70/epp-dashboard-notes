Employer Satisfaction Principal survey results on beginning teacher effectiveness

Overview

This dashboard shows results from the annual employer satisfaction survey administered each spring to principals of beginning teachers in North Carolina public schools, including charters. Principals rate each beginning teacher they supervise on a variety of teaching tasks, relative to other teachers with comparable experience.

Who Is Included

The survey goes to principals of the same teachers who receive the candidate satisfaction survey — those teaching for the first time following program completion. Prior to 2022, principals of alternate-route candidates who had not yet completed their programs also received the survey.

Data Source

Survey rosters are built by matching EPP candidate enrollment rosters to NCDPI payroll files. The survey is administered by email. The dashboard covers the most recent five years of results.

How Results Are Reported

On each item, principals rate the beginning teacher’s effectiveness relative to other teachers with comparable experience using the following scale: Not Applicable, Much Less, Less, Comparable, More, Much More. When displaying aggregated results, the dashboard reports the percentage of responses answered “Comparable,” “More,” or “Much More.”

The survey items have remained largely constant across the years covered. Seven literacy-related items were added beginning in 2021.

A copy of the most recent survey is available here: Employer Satisfaction Survey Items (PDF)

Small Counts

Results are not displayed for any group with fewer than ten survey responses. The dashboard also reports the survey response rate, disaggregated by the filters the user selects.
