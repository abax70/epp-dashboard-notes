License Exam Pass Rates Pass rates on exams required for educator licensure in North Carolina

Overview

This dashboard shows pass rates on exams required for North Carolina educator licensure. Candidates are allowed to take exams as many times as needed within their testing window, with no penalty for multiple attempts. You can learn more about license exam requirements in NC State Board of Education Policy LICN-003.

Who Is Included

Candidates pursuing initial teaching licenses (see Glossary) who completed an educator preparation program from 2015 onward. The dashboard covers exam attempts made over the most recent five years.

Data Source

Candidate rosters are submitted annually by EPPs to NCDPI. License exam data comes from two test vendors: ETS and Pearson.

Note on Pearson data: For exams administered by Pearson (e.g., edTPA, Foundations of Reading), NCDPI does not receive a direct candidate identifier. Instead, scores are matched to EPP rosters using a multi-step process based on partial SSN, name, and institution. This process matches approximately 90% of Pearson scores to the EPP rosters.

How Results Are Reported

The dashboard offers three views of pass rates, depending on the question you are trying to answer:

  • All attempts — Every exam attempt counts. If a candidate took an exam five times, all five appear.
  • First attempt — Only the candidate’s first attempt on each exam is included.
  • Best attempt — Only the candidate’s highest-scoring attempt is included. On the trend chart, results appear in the year of the best attempt, not the first.

Pass Rate by Attempt Chart

The Pass Rate by Attempt chart shows the cumulative pass rate after each attempt (first, second, third, etc.) regardless of which view you select above. Because candidates have a three-year testing window from their first attempt, this chart reaches further back in time to ensure all windows have closed for the candidates shown.

Small Counts

Results are not displayed for any group with fewer than ten candidates.
