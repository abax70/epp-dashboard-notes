License Conversions Rates at which EPP completers convert initial teaching licenses to continuing professional licenses

Overview

This dashboard shows the proportion of candidates who convert their initial teaching license to a Continuing Professional License (CPL). Depending on their pathway, candidates may progress through Residency and Initial Professional License (IPL) stages before reaching CPL.

Who Is Included

Candidates pursuing initial teaching licenses (see Glossary) who completed their EPP and received their first teaching license between 2018 and 2022. In their annual roster submissions to NCDPI, EPPs list up to three license areas each candidate is pursuing; these are used to determine whether a candidate received an initial teaching license.

How the Conversion Window Works

Once candidates receive an initial teaching license, they must convert to a CPL by the end of their third year of licensure. The conversion clock starts at licensure—not program completion. For alternate-route candidates, this means the clock may start before they finish their program.

Why doesn’t the data go up to the present? The dashboard stops short of the most recent years to allow the three-year conversion window to close. Only cohorts whose full window has elapsed are included, so the conversion rates shown are final.

Data Source

Candidate rosters are submitted annually by EPPs to NCDPI. Licensure data comes from NCDPI. See NC State Board of Education Policy LICN-001 for details on licensure requirements.

Small Counts

Results are not displayed for any group with fewer than ten candidates.
